WRITING


One bright, cold fall day many years ago, I finished reading Aldous Huxley's Brave New World for a political philosophy class. Afterward, walking through campus with my coffee, I felt thrilled and inspired, as if I'd come to the edge of something profound. I wanted to do what Huxley had done. I wanted to write a riveting novel. "A book," I thought, "that makes people think. A book that moves people." And so the idea took hold. A few years later I began writing what would become the Max Mayhem books. Whether they are moving, riveting, or both is up to the reader. They are four urban fantasy novels set in Portland, OR; a prequel followed by a trilogy. The prequel, Lune (2009), is under revision. Bloodwood (2011) is available on Goodreads and Amazon. The Oddclock (2016) is available for publication. The final book, The Garden Dark, is unfinished.



Grieving the death of his wife and leaving behind his son, Max, Dr. Mayhem seeks out and discovers the mythical Moon Gate. Recklessly, he passes through and finds himself in Lune, the land of perpetual night. Its people are neither asleep nor awake, but trapped, in thrall to the magic of a dreaming Djinn. Dr. Mayhem must pull himself together and wake the Djinn, before he, too, succumbs to the lure of dreams.

A hunt for a missing girl leads friends Max and Lydia to an eerily empty town in the cold, northern mountains of Canada. But something is not right. Prickling unease urges Max to leave. Far too late, they discover that when night falls, Bloodwood wakes. Now the missing girl is the last thing on their minds, and it will take all of Max and Lydia's cunning to survive until sunrise.

Cursed skateboarder Max Mayhem and his friend, Lydia, befriend Henry Bishop, who has a dark and secret history. When the trio learns that Professor Cornelius has been pursuing Henry's dad in a mad chase through time, it threatens to tear apart not only their friendship, but the fabric of time itself.~ 594 pages.
