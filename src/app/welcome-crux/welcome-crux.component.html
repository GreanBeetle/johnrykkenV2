c r u x

noun | ˈkrəks , ˈkru̇ks \

1 : the essential point; 2 : the main or central feature


"Crux" is my tech blog. It takes up a few pages on this website. And it has a simple goal: Explain computer, internet, and programming terms as simply as possible.


The idea for this gelled during code school. Looking through programming documentation and technical web articles, I often grew frustrated. More often than not, one technical term would be explained using additional tech terms. And that led to many a rabbit hole, seeking comprehension of one idea in order to understand another. Why, I wondered, was there no place where this stuff was written in plain English? (There are, in fact, many accessible, nicely-written pieces about tech. But these web ghosts aren't easy to find.) Anyhow, I eventually realized I could contribute, in a small way, to the ocean of tech knowledge.


I try to stick to a few rules.


  • Technical jargon is avoided where possible.
  • I assume the reader has little or no prior knowledge of the topic.
  • I imagine I am writing to a class of eighth-graders. They are computer-literate, but aren't necessesarily familiar with, say, HTML or the differnece between Linux and iOSes.
  • I keep George Orwell in mind. "Never use a long word where a short one will do," he wrote. And, "If it is possible to cut a word out, always cut it out."
